Best Practices for Form Design

April 14, 2026

The article talks a lot about many UX conceps, but there are a few things that stand out. Essentially, the idea that the perception of complexity matters as much as actual complexity is something worth thinking about. Users can scan a form before they fill it out, and that creates a lasting first impression which sets the groundwork and shapes whether they even want to try filling it out or not. That connects directly to UI/UX work because you are not just designing fields, but you are handling someone's willingness to continue in the form. The point that the article made about placeholder text being more harmful than helpful also resonated deep to me. The placeholder text disappears the moment someone starts typing, and this puts cognitive load on the user at the worst moment. Labels should be either above or even next to inputs, but not inside them.

A form that exemplifies best practices: Airbnb's booking flow In terms of a real-world form that does this well, Airbnb's booking flow is a great example. It takes a complex reservation process into easy-to-navigate, clearly labeled steps that never shows more than what is needed at one time. Additionally, the inline validation as well is used perfectly, making it a perfect example of a well-built form.
